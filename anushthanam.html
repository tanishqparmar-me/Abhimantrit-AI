Types of Anushthanam

Homa Anushthanam

Fire rituals performed with specific mantras and offerings. This powerful practice purifies the atmosphere and invokes divine energies.

Japa Anushthanam

Focused repetition of sacred mantras for a fixed number of times. This practice develops concentration and spiritual vibration.

Tirtha Anushthanam

Pilgrimage-based rituals performed at sacred river sites. Involves bathing, prayers, and offerings at holy places.

Vrata Anushthanam

Observance of religious vows with fasting and prayers. Typically performed during specific lunar phases or festivals.

How to Perform Anushthanam

1

Sankalpa (Sacred Vow)

Begin by taking a formal vow stating your intention, duration, and purpose of the Anushthanam before a deity or guru.

2

Purification

Undergo physical and mental purification through bath, fasting, and meditation to prepare for the rituals.

3

Daily Rituals

Perform prescribed rituals at fixed times each day, including mantra chanting, puja, and offerings.

4

Disciplined Lifestyle

Maintain strict discipline in diet, behavior, and thoughts throughout the Anushthanam period.

5

Conclusion (Purnahuti)

Complete the Anushthanam with final offerings, prayers, and distribution of prasad to participants.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can perform Anushthanam?

Anushthanam can be performed by anyone with sincere devotion and guidance from a knowledgeable priest or guru. Some complex rituals require initiation, but many can be performed by devoted practitioners.

What is the ideal duration for Anushthanam?

Common durations include 9 days (Navaham), 11 days (Ekadasham), 21 days (Ekavimshatim), and 41 days. The duration depends on the specific purpose and tradition being followed.

What dietary restrictions apply during Anushthanam?

Most Anushthanam require a sattvic diet: vegetarian food without onion or garlic. Some may require fasting or specific food restrictions depending on the ritual.

Can I perform Anushthanam at home?

Yes, many Anushthanam can be performed at home with proper guidance. However, certain rituals require specific arrangements or should be performed at temples under priest supervision.
